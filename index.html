Much of it is exploratory, and several of the central ideas (in particular the DNAzyme-activity, horizontal-gene-transfer, and AP-4 / tyrosine-motif immune hypotheses) are presented as explicit, testable hypotheses rather than established results — they are flagged as such throughout. Overview

This compendium documents the discovery and characterisation of a DNAzyme-like catalytic domain sequence that maps precisely to the genomes of multiple Potyviridae species, with flanking regions matching eukaryotic genes across plant, fungal, and animal kingdoms. Cross-kingdom horizontal gene transfer is the most parsimonious explanation for this pattern. Additionally, the Potyviridae CI helicase - harbouring the DNAzyme-like sequence, corresponds to a conserved protein motif likely involved in viral-host interactions associated with intracellular sorting (potentially via AP-4), viral replication, viral spread and immunity.

Key findings at a glance

  • A DNAzyme catalytic domain sequence matches perfectly to a region in the genomes of several potyviruses (6 species, 16 isolates).
  • The flanking regions of those DNAzyme-matching sequences (16 isolates, 9 identical by sequence) are identical to regions in the genomes of several eukaryotes, including plants (potential viral hosts) and insects (potential viral vectors).
  • Interactions between those eukaryotic species suggest the homology might reflect cross-kingdom horizontal DNA transfer.
  • The eukaryotic genes span diverse functions — DNA repair, transcription regulation, chromatin remodelling, RNA processing, translation, and genes of unknown function — several of them essential for cell survival and development.

All computationally intensive steps (alignment, phylogenetic inference, NCBI fetching, ELM queries) are orchestrated by a targets pipeline:

# Install the pipeline dependencies once
install.packages(c("targets", "tarchetypes"))
remotes::install_local("packages/potytools")

# Run the full pipeline (skips up-to-date steps automatically)
targets::tar_make()

# Visualise dependencies
targets::tar_visnetwork()

External tools required on PATH for running the entire pipeline: mafft, iqtree2, hmmscan.
