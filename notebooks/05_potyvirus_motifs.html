5.1 Overview

The potyvirus genome is translated as a single polyprotein that is cleaved into ~11 mature proteins, each carrying short functional signature motifs whose substitution changes transmissibility, silencing suppression, replication or movement (Revers and García 2015). This chapter (i) catalogues those motifs with their roles and primary references, (ii) locates the linear ones in the ICTV Potyviridae alignment (Inoue-Nagata et al. 2022), and (iii) draws the curated protein–protein interaction network that centres on the cylindrical inclusion (CI) helicase — the protein carrying the DNAzyme-like locus studied in the earlier chapters.

5.2 CI helicase 3D structure (predicted)

The cylindrical inclusion carries the DNAzyme-like locus and the ELM-rich CI region. No experimental potyvirus CI structure exists in the PDB and AlphaFold DB has no model for the viral polyprotein entries, so the full 644-residue TuMV CI protein (mature chain 1228–1871 of UniProt P89509) was folded with AlphaFold 3 on the AlphaFold Server (pTM = 0.78, no clashes; the ESMFold domain in scripts/fold_ci_esmfold.R is retained as an offline fallback). The viewer highlights three functional sites: the Walker-A P-loop (ATPase) and DECH box (helicase motif II) in gold, and the ELM/DNAzyme-like motif region (VASYN ± 20; res 236–280) in red.

5.5 Two host-type sorting signals in the CI region

The stretch of CI that carries the DNAzyme-like locus (the ELM-rich region anchored on VASYN in Chapter 4) turns out to sit right next to the two canonical endocytic sorting signals that adaptor-protein (AP) complexes read:

  • a tyrosine-based motif of the YXXΦ type (Φ = a bulky hydrophobic residue), and
  • immediately beside it, an acidic dileucine signal, [DE]XXXL[LI].

These are not incidental look-alikes. Wu et al. (Wu, Jia, Rui, et al. 2022) showed that the multifunctional CI protein of Turnip mosaic virus contains seven conserved acidic dileucine motifs, that these motifs are recognised by the adaptor complex AP-2, and that alanine substitutions in them reduce AP-2 binding, endosomal targeting and viral replication. In other words, the CI’s dileucine signals are functional endocytic sorting signals, not passengers. The same group further showed that TuMV co-opts the vacuolar sorting receptor VSR4 to deliver its replication vesicles to the endosome for genome replication (Wu, Jia, Ding, et al. 2022), so this endosomal targeting is central to the viral life cycle.

That matters for the adjacent tyrosine motif. Because a functionally-validated dileucine sits immediately next to it, the YXXΦ motif is very likely surface- exposed and accessible for adaptor interactions of its own — and YXXΦ is the classic ligand of the µ subunit of AP complexes (AP-1/2/3/4). Chapter 9 develops why this is interesting: a plant immune regulator (BDA1) is sorted by AP-4 through exactly this kind of tyrosine motif (Huang et al. 2026), so a virus displaying host-type Y/dileucine signals on its CI is positioned to plug into — and potentially subvert — the same endomembrane sorting machinery.

5.6 Protein–protein interaction network

A curated interaction network of the potyvirus proteins, centred on CI, drawn from the CI interactome review (Sorel et al. 2014), the HC-Pro multitasking review (Valli et al. 2018), the movement/PIPO literature (Chung et al. 2008; Xue et al. 2023) and VPg–host factor interactions (Robaglia and Caranta 2006). Host targets are added from the systematic TuMV–Arabidopsis yeast-two-hybrid interaction map (Martínez et al. 2023), in which NIb (the RdRp) was the dominant host hub (245 contacts, including salicylate-dependent immune regulators). Solid edges are curated viral–viral interactions; dashed edges are the high-throughput Y2H host interactions.

5.7 Two hubs: CI and NIb

Two viral proteins dominate the network. CI bridges replication (with VPg/NIb) and cell-to-cell movement (with CP, HC-Pro, P3N-PIPO and, via P3N-PIPO, the plasmodesmatal factor PCaP1) (Sorel et al. 2014; Xue et al. 2023) — and it carries the conserved DNAzyme-like locus and the ELM-rich CI region studied elsewhere in this book. NIb, the RdRp, is the dominant host hub in the systematic interaction map (Martínez et al. 2023), contacting salicylate-immunity regulators (NPR1, TGA1) and the SUMO machinery (SCE1) — implicating replication-complex assembly in the direct subversion of SA-dependent defence. Together these two hubs are where the sequence-level (motif/DNAzyme/ELM) and network-level views of the potyvirus proteome converge.

## Two host-type sorting signals in the CI region

The stretch of CI that carries the DNAzyme-like locus (the ELM-rich region
anchored on `VASYN` in Chapter 4) turns out to sit right next to **the two
canonical endocytic sorting signals that adaptor-protein (AP) complexes read**:

- a **tyrosine-based motif** of the **YXXΦ** type (Φ = a bulky hydrophobic
  residue), and
- immediately beside it, an **acidic dileucine signal**, `[DE]XXXL[LI]`.

These are not incidental look-alikes. Wu et al. [@wu2022ci] showed that the
multifunctional CI protein of *Turnip mosaic virus* contains **seven conserved
acidic dileucine motifs**, that these motifs are **recognised by the adaptor
complex AP-2**, and that **alanine substitutions** in them **reduce AP-2 binding,
endosomal targeting and viral replication**. In other words, the CI's dileucine
signals are *functional* endocytic sorting signals, not passengers. The same group
further showed that TuMV **co-opts the vacuolar sorting receptor VSR4 to deliver
its replication vesicles to the endosome** for genome replication [@wu2022vsr4],
so this endosomal targeting is central to the viral life cycle.

That matters for the adjacent tyrosine motif. Because a functionally-validated
dileucine sits immediately next to it, the **YXXΦ motif is very likely surface-
exposed and accessible** for adaptor interactions of its own — and YXXΦ is the
classic ligand of the µ subunit of AP complexes (AP-1/2/3/**4**). Chapter 9
develops why this is interesting: a plant immune regulator (BDA1) is sorted by
**AP-4 through exactly this kind of tyrosine motif** [@huang2026], so a virus
displaying host-type Y/dileucine signals on its CI is positioned to plug into —
and potentially subvert — the same endomembrane sorting machinery.

## Two hubs: CI and NIb

Two viral proteins dominate the network. **CI** bridges replication (with VPg/NIb)
and cell-to-cell movement (with CP, HC-Pro, P3N-PIPO and, via P3N-PIPO, the
plasmodesmatal factor PCaP1) [@sorel2014; @xue2023] — and it carries the conserved
DNAzyme-like locus and the ELM-rich CI region studied elsewhere in this book.
**NIb**, the RdRp, is the dominant *host* hub in the systematic interaction map
[@martinez2023], contacting salicylate-immunity regulators (NPR1, TGA1) and the
SUMO machinery (SCE1) — implicating replication-complex assembly in the direct
subversion of SA-dependent defence. Together these two hubs are where the
sequence-level (motif/DNAzyme/ELM) and network-level views of the potyvirus
proteome converge.
