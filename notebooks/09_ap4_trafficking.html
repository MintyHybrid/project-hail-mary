9  AP-4 Trafficking and the Tyrosine-Motif Hypothesis

Mechanistic reasoning behind the AP-4 arm of the immune trafficking model, and the tyrosine-motif hypothesis it motivates

Author

Christina Muedsam

Published

July 13, 2026

9.1 Overview

The interactive trafficking simulation foregrounds AP-4 because it is the least-studied of the four adaptor-protein complexes yet, we argue, a plausible integration point between membrane trafficking and immune signalling. This chapter sets out the mechanistic reasoning and the (largely hypothetical) cargo assignments that motivate the model’s AP-4 arm.

Much of what follows is predictive: it extrapolates from well-characterised mammalian AP-4 biology and from sorting-motif analysis to plant proteins that have not been experimentally validated as AP-4 cargo. Established facts (ATG9A as AP-4 cargo in mammals; VSR1/BOR1 as AP-4 cargo in Arabidopsis; AP-4’s requirement for the hypersensitive response) are cited; predictions are labelled as such and presented as testable hypotheses, not conclusions.

9.2 AP-4: the overlooked trafficking pathway

Adaptor protein complex 4 (AP-4) is a heterotetramer — subunits β4 (AP4B, At5g11490), ε (AP4E, At5g09420), μ4 (AP4M, At4g24550) and σ4 (AP4S, At3g13420) in Arabidopsis. Unlike AP-1/2/3, AP-4 operates without a clathrin coat and mediates export from the trans-Golgi network (TGN) to endosomal and autophagy-related compartments (Hirst et al. 2013; Fuji et al. 2016).

Complex Location Coat Function Knockout phenotype
AP-1 TGN/endosomes Clathrin TGN–endosome, secretion Embryo-lethal (mammals)
AP-2 Plasma membrane Clathrin Endocytosis Lethal (plants/mammals)
AP-3 TGN/endosomes None Vacuole/lysosome Hermansky–Pudlak (mammals)
AP-4 TGN None TGN → endosome/autophagy Neurodegeneration (mammals); subtle (plants)

The compartments AP-4 connects, and the lipid identities that mark them, are summarised below; AP-4 acts at the TGN, exporting cargo (notably ATG9) toward endosomal and autophagy compartments (dashed arrow).

AP-4 is understudied in plants precisely because it is not essential for viability and gives subtle phenotypes — yet in mammals its loss causes a hereditary spastic paraplegia (“AP-4 deficiency syndrome”) traced to missorting of its most conserved cargo, the autophagy lipid-scramblase ATG9A (Mattera et al. 2017; Davies et al. 2018). In Arabidopsis, AP-4 is experimentally established to sort the tyrosine-motif cargoes VACUOLAR SORTING RECEPTOR 1 and the borate transporter BOR1 via its µ subunit, and its loss abolishes the hypersensitive cell-death response to avirulent bacteria (Fuji et al. 2016).

9.2.1 Predicted plant AP-4 cargo

Mammalian AP-4 cargo (ATG9A, AMPA receptors, SLC17A5, SERINC1/3, δ-opioid receptor, ADAM10) are transmembrane proteins bearing [D/E]XXX[L/I/M] or YXXΦ sorting motifs and functioning in membrane remodelling, receptor localisation, autophagosome formation or lipid transport. Screening plant immune-relevant proteins for the same motifs yields a ranked set of candidate cargo — a bioinformatic prediction, not a validated interaction map:

Only the ATG9 assignment rests on strong cross-species precedent (Mattera et al. 2017; Davies et al. 2018); the immune-regulator assignments (NPR1, CBP60g, PUB22/23) are the novel, lower-confidence predictions that the callout above flags.

9.3 TEPSIN: the AP-4 accessory protein

TEPSIN is an AP-4-interacting protein that stabilises the complex, enhances membrane curvature and concentrates cargo prior to clathrin-independent scission (Hirst et al. 2013). A plant TEPSIN-like protein is present but uncharacterised (Arabidopsis candidate At5g48795). We hypothesise it stabilises AP-4 under the elevated trafficking demand of stress responses, predicting that tepsin mutants phenocopy mild ap4 defects and that TEPSIN–AP-4 association is enhanced during infection. 9.4 NPR1 and CBP60g: trafficking hypotheses for immune regulators

NPR1, the master regulator of systemic acquired resistance (SAR), is held as cytoplasmic oligomers that must be reduced to monomers — via thioredoxins and S-nitrosylation at Cys156 — before nuclear import and coactivation of PR genes (Mou et al. 2003; Tada et al. 2008; Kinkema et al. 2000). The compartment in which monomerisation occurs is not fully resolved. We propose (hypothesis) that AP-4 routes NPR1 oligomers through an endosomal compartment whose reducing environment promotes monomerisation before retrograde delivery and nuclear import:

This predicts that ap4 mutants show altered NPR1 localisation and SA-response kinetics, testable by ap4 npr1 epistasis. Analogously, CBP60g — a calcium-responsive transcription factor that activates the SA-biosynthesis gene ICS1 (Wang et al. 2009) — may be held in a vesicular pool and released for rapid perinuclear import upon MAMP-triggered Ca²⁺ influx, with AP-4 providing the sorting step; ap4 mutants would then show delayed CBP60g nuclear accumulation and reduced SA after infection.

ImportantA published AP-4 → immunity link — and a hidden thread to NPR1

The NPR1/CBP60g routes above remain predictions. But there is now a concrete, published AP-4↔︎immunity mechanism that makes the whole idea far less speculative. Huang et al. (2026) showed that the AP-4 µ subunit binds the transmembrane immune protein BDA1 through BDA1’s tyrosine residues (Y18, Y257), and that AP-4 is required for SNC2-mediated immunity (a receptor-like-protein pathway) as well as for basal, pattern- and effector-triggered immunity; a second endocytic adaptor, ECA4, tunes BDA1 levels in parallel (Wang et al. 2025). This is a direct precedent for a plant defence protein being AP-4-sorted via a tyrosine-based motif — precisely the motif class the viral CI displays (Chapter 5).

The thread to NPR1 is suggestive but not yet fully characterised: SNC2 is literally SUPPRESSOR OF NPR1-1, CONSTITUTIVE 2 — i.e. it was isolated genetically in relation to NPR1 and defines an RLP-based resistance branch that runs partly parallel to / independent of NPR1. So AP-4, by gating BDA1/SNC2, sits on an immune axis genetically tied to NPR1, without (to my knowledge) a demonstrated direct AP-4–NPR1 interaction. We flag this as a hypothesis worth pursuing, not an established pathway.

9.5 Cargo missorting as a distinct pathology

The central hypothesis reframes AP-4 deficiency: the damaging consequence may be not failed degradation but aberrant localisation, producing a signalling imbalance. Missorting can create autophagy-resistant protein aggregates and mislocalised signalling components — a route to chronic, autoimmune-like activation that is mechanistically distinct from simple loss of function. Several independent routes converge on this outcome, paralleling known plant autoimmune mutants (e.g. ESCRT-defective vps alleles that fail to degrade activated receptors; autophagy-deficient backgrounds that accumulate NLRs):

  • Failed receptor degradation → receptor persistence at the PM → chronic MAPK.
  • NLR hyperaccumulation in autophagy-deficient backgrounds → ETI without effector.
  • Persistent endosomal signalling platforms when ESCRT sorting fails.
  • Lipid-identity imbalance (PI(3)P/PI(3,5)P₂ dysregulation) → trafficking paralysis and, at the extreme, the membrane-topology inversion tracked by the simulation (Simon et al. 2016).
  • Cargo missorting → aggregates that resist autophagy and trigger stress signalling.

These mechanisms are why the model reads out an autoimmune-risk signal (reversed-topology exosomes) rather than a simple loss-of-signal, and why the AP-4 arm — despite AP-4’s subtle single-mutant phenotype — is worth modelling.

9.6 Synthesis: a tyrosine-motif hypothesis

Pulling the threads together, the working hypothesis of this compendium is not about DNAzyme catalysis at all — it is about sorting motifs. Four propositions, in decreasing order of support:

  1. The CI’s tyrosine/dileucine motifs let the virus tap host sorting. The potyvirus CI carries functional acidic-dileucine signals read by AP-2 (Wu et al. 2022) and, beside them, an accessible YXXΦ tyrosine motif (Chapter 5). A cytosolic pathogen protein wearing host-type endocytic signals is positioned to co-opt the AP-mediated sorting machinery it needs for replication and movement — and, potentially, to perturb the immune trafficking that runs on the same adaptors.
  2. Tyrosine-based sorting is a deeply conserved, re-used device. YXXΦ (and the related immunoreceptor tyrosine motifs, ITAM/ITIM) govern receptor sorting and signalling from plants to mammals; the same motif logic is repeatedly exploited by human-pathogenic viruses, including RNA viruses and oncoviruses. That AP-4 itself reads a tyrosine motif on the plant immune protein BDA1 (Huang et al. 2026) shows the device is active on both sides of the host–pathogen interface.
  3. Membrane-topology reversal can originate only at the LE/MVB. The late-endosome / multivesicular body is the branch point where sorting, recycling, autophagy and degradation segregate — and the only compartment where vesicles are generated by membrane remodelling away from the cytosol (ILV budding). A defect that scrambles this orchestration is therefore the only plausible origin of the reversed-topology, cytoplasm-facing vesicles the simulation flags as an autoimmune risk. AP-4 is implicated at exactly this hub, from ATG9 delivery for autophagy to the vacuole–PM fusion that executes HR cell death (Hatsugai et al. 2018).
  4. Tyrosine motifs, modified, are read across the whole endomembrane system. The same Y/dileucine signals — and their phospho-/other modifications — feed autophagy (the CI region’s ELM hits include autophagy-related targeting classes; Chapter 4), ESCRT-dependent ILV sorting, and AP-complex recognition. For TuMV in particular, both NPR1-related resistance and autophagy are established parts of the host-defence landscape (Wu et al. 2024), and viral replication itself runs on endomembrane co-option (Jovanović et al. 2023). A fuller review of how these modifications switch a motif between sorting fates is the natural next chapter of this story.

Everything past proposition 1 is explicitly hypothesis; the value of the interactive model is to make those hypotheses quantitative and falsifiable rather than merely narrative.

```{mermaid}
  E -- Yes --> F[Aberrant autophagosomes:<br/>ATG8+ membranes, poor cargo recruitment]
  F --> G[Non-selective autophagy impaired]
  F --> H[Selective NBR1 autophagy compensatorily enhanced]
  F --> I[Chronic autophagy-stress response]
```

## NPR1 and CBP60g: trafficking hypotheses for immune regulators

NPR1, the master regulator of systemic acquired resistance (SAR), is held as
cytoplasmic oligomers that must be reduced to monomers — via thioredoxins and
S-nitrosylation at Cys156 — before nuclear import and coactivation of *PR* genes
[@mou2003; @tada2008; @kinkema2000]. The compartment in which monomerisation
occurs is not fully resolved. We propose (hypothesis) that AP-4 routes NPR1
oligomers through an endosomal compartment whose reducing environment promotes
monomerisation before retrograde delivery and nuclear import:

```{mermaid}
%%| label: fig-npr1-hypothesis
%%| fig-cap: "Hypothesised AP-4-dependent endosomal step in NPR1 activation (predicted, not established). AP-4 loss could either reduce nuclear NPR1 (loss of function) or, if the compartment normally sequesters NPR1, increase it (gain of function/autoimmunity)."
flowchart LR
  A[NPR1 oligomers<br/>cytoplasm] --> B[AP-4-mediated<br/>TGN sorting]
  B --> C[Endosomal compartment<br/>reducing environment]
  C --> D[Monomerisation]
  D --> E[Retrograde to cytosol]
  E --> F[Nuclear import → PR genes]
  B -. AP-4 loss .-> G[Missorted / sequestered NPR1]
  G --> H[Reduced SAR<br/>or constitutive autoimmunity]
```

This predicts that *ap4* mutants show altered NPR1 localisation and SA-response
kinetics, testable by *ap4 npr1* epistasis. Analogously, CBP60g — a
calcium-responsive transcription factor that activates the SA-biosynthesis gene
*ICS1* [@wang2009cbp60g] — may be held in a vesicular pool and released for rapid
perinuclear import upon MAMP-triggered Ca²⁺ influx, with AP-4 providing the
sorting step; *ap4* mutants would then show delayed CBP60g nuclear accumulation
and reduced SA after infection.

::: {.callout-important appearance="simple"}
## A published AP-4 → immunity link — and a hidden thread to NPR1
The NPR1/CBP60g routes above remain **predictions**. But there is now a
**concrete, published** AP-4↔immunity mechanism that makes the whole idea far
less speculative. @huang2026 showed that the **AP-4 µ subunit binds the
transmembrane immune protein BDA1 through BDA1's tyrosine residues (Y18, Y257)**,
and that AP-4 is required for **SNC2-mediated immunity** (a receptor-like-protein
pathway) as well as for basal, pattern- and effector-triggered immunity; a second
endocytic adaptor, ECA4, tunes BDA1 levels in parallel [@wang2025eca4]. This is a
**direct precedent for a plant defence protein being AP-4-sorted via a
tyrosine-based motif** — precisely the motif class the viral CI displays
(Chapter 5).

The thread to **NPR1** is suggestive but **not yet fully characterised**: SNC2 is
literally *SUPPRESSOR OF NPR1-1, CONSTITUTIVE 2* — i.e. it was isolated genetically
in relation to NPR1 and defines an RLP-based resistance branch that runs partly
**parallel to / independent of NPR1**. So AP-4, by gating BDA1/SNC2, sits on an
immune axis genetically tied to NPR1, without (to my knowledge) a demonstrated
direct AP-4–NPR1 interaction. We flag this as a **hypothesis worth pursuing**, not
an established pathway.
:::

## Cargo missorting as a distinct pathology

The central hypothesis reframes AP-4 deficiency: the damaging consequence may be
not *failed degradation* but **aberrant localisation**, producing a signalling
imbalance. Missorting can create autophagy-resistant protein aggregates and
mislocalised signalling components — a route to chronic, autoimmune-like
activation that is mechanistically distinct from simple loss of function. Several
independent routes converge on this outcome, paralleling known plant autoimmune
mutants (e.g. ESCRT-defective *vps* alleles that fail to degrade activated
receptors; autophagy-deficient backgrounds that accumulate NLRs):

- **Failed receptor degradation** → receptor persistence at the PM → chronic MAPK.
- **NLR hyperaccumulation** in autophagy-deficient backgrounds → ETI without effector.
- **Persistent endosomal signalling platforms** when ESCRT sorting fails.
- **Lipid-identity imbalance** (PI(3)P/PI(3,5)P₂ dysregulation) → trafficking paralysis and, at the extreme, the membrane-topology inversion tracked by the simulation [@simon2016].
- **Cargo missorting → aggregates** that resist autophagy and trigger stress signalling.

These mechanisms are why the model reads out an *autoimmune-risk* signal
(reversed-topology exosomes) rather than a simple loss-of-signal, and why the
AP-4 arm — despite AP-4's subtle single-mutant phenotype — is worth modelling.

## Synthesis: a tyrosine-motif hypothesis

Pulling the threads together, the working hypothesis of this compendium is not
about DNAzyme catalysis at all — it is about **sorting motifs**. Four propositions,
in decreasing order of support:

1. **The CI's tyrosine/dileucine motifs let the virus tap host sorting.** The
   potyvirus CI carries functional acidic-dileucine signals read by AP-2
   [@wu2022ci] and, beside them, an accessible **YXXΦ tyrosine motif** (Chapter 5).
   A cytosolic pathogen protein wearing host-type endocytic signals is positioned
   to **co-opt the AP-mediated sorting machinery** it needs for replication and
   movement — and, potentially, to perturb the immune trafficking that runs on the
   same adaptors.
2. **Tyrosine-based sorting is a deeply conserved, re-used device.** YXXΦ (and the
   related immunoreceptor tyrosine motifs, ITAM/ITIM) govern receptor sorting and
   signalling from plants to mammals; the same motif logic is repeatedly exploited
   by human-pathogenic viruses, including RNA viruses and oncoviruses. That AP-4
   itself reads a tyrosine motif on the plant immune protein **BDA1** [@huang2026]
   shows the device is active on *both* sides of the host–pathogen interface.
3. **Membrane-topology reversal can originate only at the LE/MVB.** The
   late-endosome / multivesicular body is the branch point where sorting,
   recycling, autophagy and degradation segregate — and the **only** compartment
   where vesicles are generated by membrane remodelling *away from* the cytosol
   (ILV budding). A defect that scrambles this orchestration is therefore the only
   plausible origin of the reversed-topology, cytoplasm-facing vesicles the
   simulation flags as an autoimmune risk. AP-4 is implicated at exactly this hub,
   from ATG9 delivery for autophagy to the vacuole–PM fusion that executes HR cell
   death [@hatsugai2018].
4. **Tyrosine motifs, modified, are read across the whole endomembrane system.**
   The same Y/dileucine signals — and their phospho-/other modifications — feed
   **autophagy** (the CI region's ELM hits include autophagy-related targeting
   classes; Chapter 4), **ESCRT**-dependent ILV sorting, and **AP-complex**
   recognition. For TuMV in particular, both **NPR1-related resistance and
   autophagy** are established parts of the host-defence landscape [@wu2024brassica],
   and viral replication itself runs on endomembrane co-option [@jovanovic2023]. A
   fuller review of how these modifications switch a motif between sorting fates is
   the natural next chapter of this story.

Everything past proposition 1 is explicitly **hypothesis**; the value of the
[interactive model](08_immu_simmu.qmd) is to make those hypotheses *quantitative
and falsifiable* rather than merely narrative.
